# Overview
This report analyzes a suspicious email and associated URL received on September 7, 2023, at 5:33 PM, claiming a transaction of 0.79 ETH via the Ethereum network. The email appears to be a phishing attempt, and the linked URL has been flagged as malicious.



# Email Analysis

Sender: noreply@firesonic.ca

Recipients:
Multiple recipients including tiny23ian@hotmail.co.uk, henterprize@hotmail.com, phishing@pot.o, admichael@hotmail.co.uk, achmed_99@hotmail.com, nsprasla@hotmail.com, donovantokarjo@hotmail.com

# Content:
Claims a transaction of 0.79 ETH was sent via the Ethereum network.
Includes a "CANCEL TRANSACTIONS" button and a request to verify a phone number to protect against fraud.
Dated September 9, 2023, at 6:31 PM, which contradicts the email timestamp (September 7, 2023).
Copyright notice from Coinbase (© 2023 Coinbase, All rights reserved), suggesting impersonation.

# Red Flags:
-Multiple unrelated recipients, a common tactic in phishing emails.

-Inconsistent dates between the email header and content.

-Urgency to "cancel transactions" with a verification request, a typical phishing lure.



# URL Analysis
URL: https://firesonic.ca/404.html

Source: Linked within the email, analyzed via VirusTotal.


# Findings:

VirusTotal flagged the URL as malicious by 1 out of 97 security vendors (Webroot).
Status: 200 (text/html), last analyzed a moment ago.
Other vendors (e.g., Acronis, AILabs, BitDefender) marked it as clean, but the single malicious flag warrants caution.
The URL leads to a 404 Page Not Found error, suggesting the site may be a decoy or has been taken down.

Red Flags:
Discrepancy between malicious flag and clean verdicts may indicate evolving threats.
404 error could imply the site was designed to evade detection or has been abandoned post-attack.

# Conclusion and Recommendations
The email and associated URL exhibit characteristics of a phishing attempt:

-Impersonation of Coinbase with inconsistent dates.

-Use of a potentially malicious URL.

-Social engineering tactics to prompt action (e.g., canceling transactions).

-Recommendations
-
-Do Not Interact: Avoid clicking links or providing personal information.

-Report: Notify Coinbase and relevant authorities about the phishing attempt.

-Monitor: Watch for similar emails and secure accounts with two-factor authentication.

Further Investigation: Consider deeper analysis with updated threat intelligence if the domain reappears.
